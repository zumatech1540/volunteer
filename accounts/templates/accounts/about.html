Profile Summary

Ndiritu Muriithi is a seasoned economist and financial markets expert with over 35 years of experience across international finance, governance, industrial development, and public policy in Kenya, South Africa, Canada, and Australia.

He previously served as the 2nd Governor of Laikipia County, where he pioneered innovative financing models including leasing structures for equipment acquisition and became the first county leader in Kenya to explore infrastructure bond financing for long-term development funding.

His leadership introduced the Smart Towns Initiative, modernizing urban planning, infrastructure, and investment readiness across Laikipia. He also expanded rural road networks to improve trade, mobility, and access to essential services.

In economic development, he introduced SME support programs, manufacturer rebate systems, and interest subsidy financing in partnership with KCB Bank Kenya to stimulate business growth and industrialization.

In healthcare, his administration strengthened medical infrastructure and expanded access to quality healthcare services across the county.

He also spearheaded a comprehensive water masterplan, delivering flagship projects such as the Solio Water Project and Makurian Water Project, extending clean piped water to communities over 70km away.

Earlier, as Member of Parliament for Laikipia West and Assistant Minister for Industrialization, he championed SME reforms, credit information sharing laws, and education expansion programs.

He sponsored the establishment of 23 secondary schools, 3 youth polytechnics, and a university in Laikipia West, significantly improving education access.

He also led national peace-building initiatives including the Laikipia Peace Caravan, covering 32 districts and reducing inter-community conflict between 2009–2012.

Before public office, he worked at the International Finance Corporation (IFC), where he helped design financial systems including credit bureaus, mortgage markets, SME banking systems, and women-enterprise financing programs across Africa.

Currently, he supports over 10 counties in Kenya in SME development and Own Source Revenue (OSR) optimization and mentors entrepreneurs through the Ujuzi na Biashara Trust.
